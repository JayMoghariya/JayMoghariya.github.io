Types of Orders

  1. CNC/Delivery - Cash and Carry : Buy and Sell Delivery Orders
  2. MIS/Intraday - Margin Intraday Square-Off : Buy and Sell Intraday Orders
  3. Limit Order
  4. Market Order
  5. Stop Loss Market Order
  6. Stop Loss Limit Order
  7. Cover Order: Cover order covers the loss, so it has only stop-loss no target.
  8. Bracket Order: Bracket order has target as well as stoploss. If the target gets hit the stop loss order will be cancelled automatically.
  9. Trailing Stop Loss: Trailing stop-loss can be set at a percentage => as underlying price rises the stoploss also rises maintaining a distance/percentage. However, if the market price drops, the stop price remains unchanged. Visit for more details
  10. After Market Order(AMO): a sort of order that can be placed after regular trading hours and is executed once the market opens
  11. OCO - One Cancels the Other: One-cancels-the-other (OCO) is a type of conditional order for a pair of orders in which the execution of one automatically cancels the other. Generally used for volatile stocks. e.g. You may have a Sell target with, say, 10% profit at Rs 709.5 or a Loss target of, say 10% at Rs 508.5. You want only one of these order to execute in this case.
  12. Iceberg Order: Iceberg is an order type that slices orders of larger quantity (or value) into smaller orders, where each small order, or leg, is sent to the exchange only after the previous order is filled.
  13. Immediate or Cancel (IOC): An immediate-or-cancel order (IOC order) is one which has to be executed immediately and fully, or as fully as possible. Non-executed parts of an IOC order are deleted without entry in the order book.
  14. Fill or Kill (FOK): A fill-or-kill order is an order to buy or sell a stock that must be executed immediately in its entirety; otherwise, the entire order will be cancelled (i.e., no partial execution of the order is allowed).
